About

About Me

I’m Tango Hunter—an older, chill dude who believes gaming should be fun, not stressful. I’m a dad first, doing my best to balance real life with some well-earned time in front of the screen. Whether I’m exploring new worlds or revisiting old favorites, I bring a laid-back vibe, a bit of sarcasm, and plenty of laughs. I’m not here to be the best—I’m here to show up, have a good time, and enjoy the ride (even when things go sideways).

Schedule

I stream weeknights from 9 PM to midnight EST, keeping things relaxed and fun. On the first Wednesday of each month, I switch gears for Tech Night—stepping away from games to dive into the digital world with tutorials and hands-on projects. Whether you’re here to learn something new or just hang out, there’s always something going on. Just a heads up: the schedule can change without notice for family time or a little personal decompression.

Games

My regular streams are all about playing whatever feels right in the moment. Some nights we’re kicking back with cozy games like Palia or Minecraft, other nights we’re diving into something spooky like Phasmophobia. I mix in FPS games, RPG adventures, and the occasional wildcard just to keep things interesting. No matter what we’re playing, the goal is always the same—relax, have fun, and enjoy the ride.

Socials

Discord

If you want the most up-to-date info, my Discord is the place to be. That’s where I post schedule updates, announcements, and anything happening behind the scenes. It’s also a great spot to hang out with the community and keep the conversation going off stream. Life happens and plans change, so if you ever need the latest details, Discord’s got you covered.

Missed a Tech Night or want to go back through something at your own pace? My YouTube is where all those streams live after the fact. You’ll find tutorials, walkthroughs, and deep dives into whatever we explored. It’s all there whenever you need it—no live schedule required.

Github

Looking for the code from the streams? My GitHub is where I keep all the repositories. From Tech Night projects to random experiments, everything gets posted there so you can follow along, tinker, or dig into how things work. It’s all organized and ready whenever you are.
